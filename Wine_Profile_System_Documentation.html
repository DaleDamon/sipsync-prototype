Executive Summary

The SipSync Wine Profile System is a sophisticated classification framework designed to identify and categorize wine consumer preferences through a 15-question interactive quiz. The system analyzes user responses to everyday food and drink questions to classify individuals into one of 10 distinct wine consumer profiles, each with unique characteristic preferences for acidity, tannins, body weight, sweetness, and flavor profiles.

Rather than asking users directly about wine (which requires expertise), the quiz asks about familiar foods and beverages that naturally correlate with wine preferences. This approach makes wine discovery accessible to both novices and experts.

System Overview & Design Principles

Core Philosophy

The wine profile system is built on three fundamental principles:

  1. Accessibility: Questions focus on everyday foods and drinks that users encounter regularly, requiring no wine knowledge
  2. Scientific Basis: Each question maps to legitimate wine characteristics (acidity, tannins, body, sweetness) that are measurable and industry-standard
  3. Comprehensive Coverage: The 10 profiles span the full spectrum of wine types and consumer preferences across red, white, sparkling, and dessert wines

The 5 Wine Dimensions

All profiles are defined by five key wine characteristics:

  • Acidity: The brightness and tartness level (Low, Medium, High)
  • Tannins: The drying sensation in the mouth (Low, Medium, High) - mainly relevant for red wines
  • Body Weight: The fullness/weight of the wine on the palate (Light, Medium, Full)
  • Sweetness: Residual sugar level (Dry, Medium, Sweet)
  • Flavor Profile: Dominant flavor characteristics (oak, spice, cherry, citrus, floral, berry, vanilla, etc.)

Design Reasoning

These five dimensions were selected because they are: (1) scientifically measurable in wines, (2) independent enough to provide meaningful distinction, (3) directly perceptible to consumers, and (4) sufficient to narrow down wine recommendations to a manageable set while maintaining personalization.

The 10 Wine Consumer Profiles

Red Wine Profiles (4)

1. Full-Bodied Red Enthusiast

Wine Type: Red

Characteristics: High tannins, full body, intense flavors, dry

Flavor Notes: Oak, spice, blackberry

Example Wines: Cabernet Sauvignon, Malbec, Barolo

Consumer Profile: Likes strong coffee, dark chocolate, grilled steaks

Why This Profile Exists

This profile targets consumers who enjoy intense, structured wines with pronounced tannins. They prefer bold flavors and aren't intimidated by the astringent sensation tannins create. Their preference for strong coffee and dark chocolate indicates they enjoy complex, intense flavors without sweetness.

2. Medium-Bodied Red Aficionado

Wine Type: Red

Characteristics: Low tannins, medium body, fruity, medium sweetness

Flavor Notes: Cherry, berry, vanilla

Example Wines: Pinot Noir, Merlot, Grenache

Consumer Profile: Likes milk chocolate, fruit smoothies, roasted chicken

Why This Profile Exists

This is the most broadly appealing red wine profile. It bridges the gap between those intimidated by high tannins and those seeking maximum complexity. The preference for milk chocolate and fruit smoothies indicates comfort with both sweetness and fruit-forward flavors, making them approachable wines that don't require the same aging or food pairing sophistication as bold reds.

3. Spiced Red Connoisseur

Wine Type: Red

Characteristics: Medium tannins, medium-full body, spicy notes, dry

Flavor Notes: Spice, cherry, oak

Example Wines: Syrah/Shiraz, Zinfandel, Tempranillo

Consumer Profile: Likes BBQ sauce, black pepper, grilled meats

Why This Profile Exists

Consumers in this profile have distinct preferences for wines with peppery, spicy characteristics. Their affinity for BBQ sauce and black pepper indicates they enjoy smoky, peppery flavors. They prefer wines with more structure than the Aficionado but appreciate spice complexity over pure tannin intensity.

4. Light-Bodied Red Devotee

Wine Type: Red

Characteristics: Low tannins, light body, delicate flavors, dry

Flavor Notes: Berry, floral, citrus

Example Wines: Beaujolais, light Pinot Noir, Gamay

Consumer Profile: Likes fruit teas, light salads, delicate foods

Why This Profile Exists

This profile serves consumers who enjoy wine but prefer lighter, more delicate expressions. Their preference for fruit teas and light salads indicates they're seeking refreshment and elegance rather than intensity. This profile is often appealing to white wine drinkers transitioning to reds, or those with refined palates seeking subtlety.

Quiz Question Mapping to Profiles

Each of the 15 quiz questions is strategically designed to probe one or more of the five wine dimensions. Here's the complete breakdown:

Questions 1-3: Tannins (Astringency/Dryness)

These questions identify comfort level with drying sensations and intensity preferences.

Q1: Coffee Preference

Question: "How do you like your coffee?"

A) Black, no sugar ‚Üí High tannin preference (Full-Bodied Red Enthusiast, Spiced Red Connoisseur)

B) With cream and sugar ‚Üí Lower tannin preference (Medium-Bodied Red Aficionado)

C) I prefer tea or juice ‚Üí Avoid tannins, seek brightness (Crisp White, Sparkling, Light Red)

Why This Question Works

Black coffee without sugar provides a familiar reference point for tannin astringency. The tannin sensation in wine is similar to the bitter, drying mouthfeel of strong black coffee. Cream and sugar indicate palatability preferences, while avoiding coffee entirely suggests those seeking gentler flavors.

Q2: Chocolate Choice

Question: "Dark chocolate or milk chocolate?"

A) Dark chocolate (85%+ cacao) ‚Üí High tannins (Full-Bodied Red Enthusiast)

B) Milk chocolate ‚Üí Medium tannins (Medium-Bodied Red Aficionado)

C) White chocolate or fruity candy ‚Üí Low tannins, sweet preference (White Wines, Dessert)

Why This Question Works

Dark chocolate's bitter, astringent sensation mirrors high-tannin red wines. This is a very direct proxy for tannin tolerance. Milk chocolate's sweetness and creaminess appeals to those seeking softer, more approachable wines. White chocolate and fruity candy preferences indicate those seeking sweetness without astringency.

Q3: Tea Preference

Question: "What's your go-to tea?"

A) Black tea, unsweetened ‚Üí High tannins (Full-Bodied Red Enthusiast)

B) Green tea or herbal tea ‚Üí Medium tannins (Spiced Red Connoisseur, Aromatic White)

C) Fruit tea or sweet iced tea ‚Üí Sweet preference (Dessert Wine, Aromatic White)

Why This Question Works

Black tea is high in tannins, providing another familiar reference. Green tea offers moderate tannins with grass/vegetal notes. Herbal teas offer low tannins. Sweet iced tea clearly indicates sweet preference. This question provides a second validation of tannin preferences.

Questions 4-6: Acidity (Brightness/Tartness)

These questions measure affinity for bright, acidic sensations versus preference for softness.

Q4: Citrus Enjoyment

Question: "How do you feel about sour/tangy flavors?"

A) Love them! Gimme all the citrus ‚Üí High acidity (Crisp White, Sparkling)

B) Like them in moderation ‚Üí Medium acidity (Fruit-Forward White, Aromatic White)

C) Prefer milder, less tangy flavors ‚Üí Low acidity (Full-Bodied White, Full-Bodied Red)

Why This Question Works

This directly measures acidity preference through a universal food experience. Citrus acidity in beverages directly correlates to wine acidity perception. Those who love tartness will appreciate bright, high-acid wines. Those avoiding tartness prefer softer, rounder wines.

Q5: Salad Dressing

Question: "What salad dressing do you prefer?"

A) Vinaigrette or lemon juice ‚Üí High acidity (Crisp White, Sparkling)

B) Ranch or Caesar ‚Üí Medium acidity (Medium-Bodied Red, Fruit-Forward White)

C) Creamy or oil-based ‚Üí Low acidity (Full-Bodied White)

Why This Question Works

Vinaigrette's acidity is a daily food reference point. This is a behavioral/preference question that indicates actual choices, making it highly predictive. Creamy dressings suggest those who avoid brightness, while vinegar-based suggests those seeking it. This is the second acidity validation question.

Q6: Morning Drink

Question: "What's your ideal morning drink?"

A) Orange juice or grapefruit juice ‚Üí High acidity (Sparkling, Crisp White)

B) Smoothie or fruit blend ‚Üí Medium acidity (Aromatic White)

C) Hot chocolate or creamy latte ‚Üí Low acidity (Full-Bodied White, Dessert)

Why This Question Works

Morning drink choices reveal consistent flavor preferences. OJ/grapefruit juice drinkers actively seek acidic refreshment. Smoothies indicate comfort with fruit-forward medium flavors. Hot chocolate/latte drinkers show preference for creamy, rounded flavors. This provides third acidity confirmation.

Questions 7-9: Body Weight (Intensity/Richness)

These questions measure comfort with rich, full-bodied foods versus light, delicate preferences.

Q7: Meal Preference

Question: "Which meal sounds most appealing?"

A) Grilled steak with bold sauce ‚Üí Full body (Full-Bodied Red Enthusiast)

B) Roasted chicken with herbs ‚Üí Medium body (Spiced Red, Fruit-Forward White)

Why This Question Works

Meal richness is a direct proxy for body weight preference. Steak drinkers need full-bodied wines to complement the richness. Light fish indicates preference for lighter wines. Chicken sits in the middle. This is the most direct body-weight indicator question.

Q8: Soup Choice

Question: "What soup do you crave?"

A) Rich, creamy chowder or bisque ‚Üí Full body (Full-Bodied Red, Full-Bodied White)

B) Tomato soup or minestrone ‚Üí Medium body (Medium-Bodied Red, Aromatic White)

C) Clear broth or light gazpacho ‚Üí Light body (Crisp White, Sparkling)

Why This Question Works

Soup preferences reveal comfort with richness vs. lightness. Creamy soups appeal to those seeking full-bodied wines. Clear broths appeal to those wanting lightness. This provides a second validation of body-weight preferences through a different food context.

Q9: Pasta Sauce

Question: "What pasta sauce is your favorite?"

A) Hearty meat sauce or Bolognese ‚Üí Full body (Full-Bodied Red Enthusiast)

Why This Question Works

Pasta sauce richness is another daily-life indicator of body-weight preference. Bolognese is rich and meaty (full-bodied wine match). Tomato-based is moderate. Light oil is delicate. This third validation question covers yet another food category, improving accuracy.

Questions 10-11: Sweetness Tolerance

Dessert frequency is one of the strongest indicators of residual sugar tolerance. Dessert-lovers will enjoy sweet wines. Savory-preference people clearly need dry wines. This is the most direct sweetness indicator and serves as the primary sweetness-preference filter.

Scoring Algorithm & Reasoning

How the Algorithm Works

The quiz uses a sophisticated point-based scoring system where each answer awards points to one or more of the 10 profiles. The algorithm works as follows:

  1. Initialize Scores: Each of the 10 profiles starts with 0 points
  2. Process Answers: For each of the 15 questions, the user's answer triggers points for relevant profiles
  3. Award Points: Each answer awards 1-3 points to various profiles based on the answer's relevance
    • 3 points = strong indicator of that profile
    • 2 points = moderate indicator of that profile
    • 1 point = weak indicator of that profile
  4. Calculate Winner: After all 15 questions, the profile with the highest total score wins
  5. Apply Tiebreaker: If two profiles have equal scores, tiebreaker rules apply (see below)

Point Distribution Philosophy

Points are assigned based on how strongly each answer indicates a profile preference. For example:

Question Answer Profile Points Reasoning
Q1: Coffee A) Black, no sugar Full-Bodied Red Enthusiast 3 Very strong indicator of high tannin tolerance
A) Black, no sugar Spiced Red Connoisseur 1 Moderate indicator, not specifically about spice
B) Cream and sugar Medium-Bodied Red Aficionado 3 Clear softening preference suggests medium tannins

Why Three-Tier Scoring?

Using three point levels (1, 2, 3) instead of binary (0/1) or continuous scoring allows the algorithm to:

  • Capture gradations of relevance (exact match vs. adjacent profiles vs. weak connection)
  • Allow secondary profiles to benefit from answers that aren't their primary indicator
  • Reduce noise while maintaining sensitivity to answer patterns
  • Keep the total point range manageable (0-90 points maximum = 15 questions √ó 6 max points per question)

Tiebreaker Rules

If two profiles have equal final scores, the following tiebreaker rules apply in order:

  1. Red vs. White Tiebreaker: If a red and white profile are tied, check Q10-Q11 (dessert/sweetness preference) to distinguish
  2. Same-Category Tiebreaker: If two reds or two whites are tied, examine their flavor-profile answers (Q12-Q14)
  3. Final Tiebreaker: If still tied, default to the most versatile profile (Medium-Bodied Red Aficionado for reds, Crisp & Acidic White Enthusiast for whites)

Why These Rules?

Tiebreaker rules provide rational defaults when user responses don't clearly distinguish between profiles. The most versatile profiles are chosen as defaults because they:

  • Appeal to the broadest consumer base
  • Work with the widest variety of foods
  • Offer the least likelihood of user dissatisfaction
  • Provide excellent foundation for users to then refine preferences manually
